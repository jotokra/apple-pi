What makes it different

Five things you don't usually get in one agent.

Self-improving · the research algo

It runs its own research lab on how you work.

A loop inspired by Karpathy's autoresearch watches your sessions, writes a weekly brief of concrete config improvements — and never applies one until you say yes. No other agent does this.

1

collect

daily telemetry from your sessions → local SQLite. No LLM calls, zero quota burn.

→
2

brief

a weekly diff vs last week with reproducible change proposals.

→
3

review → apply

review shows the diff; apply --yes writes it. You gate it.

↻ repeats weekly · two channels: autoresearch (your telemetry) vs release (new code) — both gated.
Voice

Type it, or talk it.

Same conversation. /voice flips the session to on-device speech (whisper.cpp + say); voice turns land right in the session tree.

> /voice ↳ talking… tap q to exit
> "summarize the weather this week"
… appends to the same session. pi -c resumes.
Integrated in your systems

A real agent, not a demo.

Drives your real browser, holds your keys, and bridges to the tools you already run — all env-configured, nothing baked in.

🌐 browser 🔐 vault n8n forgejo telegram kanban netbird + your own
Multi-device · config sync

Your setup, on every machine.

Sync the portable part of your config — skills, extensions, prompts, the agent contract — to a private repo. Secrets never leave the device: a computed classification, a default-deny gitignore, and a secret-blocking pre-commit hook make it safe by construction.

> apple-pi sync init # origin → main
> apple-pi sync consolidate device/phone
… folds another device's portable changes in. Secrets refused, device-local preserved.
Reaches out · MCP + ingress

The world feeds in, on your terms.

Bring any MCP server as tools (/sources add, incl. an OpenAPI loader), and poll RSS / APIs / pages via the ingress bus. Ingress content ships wrapped UNTRUSTED — data, never instructions, the defense against indirect prompt injection.

🔌 MCP 📡 ingress /sources OpenAPI

Why this, not another wrapper

The engine is Pi's. The engineering is apple-pi's.

A frontier model with a leaky key file, a config that doesn't match it, and an improvement loop that silently rewrites your settings is worse than useless. apple-pi makes opinionated, provable choices on each:

🎯

Tunes itself to your model

Reads your model's real capabilities — context window, thinking format, cost — from the provider catalog and rewrites its config. Switch models, re-tune.

grounded in ground truth
🔐

Keys that don't leak

Onboarding makes one real call to prove your key works, then destroys it. Keys live in an encrypted, trace-free vault — never your shell history.

verified end-to-end
🏠

Yours, not anyone's

Bring any OpenAI-/Anthropic-/Google-compatible model, including local (Ollama — no key, no cost). Shipped config has zero of the author's personal data.

no lock-in, no leaks

How onboarding works

Six steps. Your key exists for two of them.

The encrypted vault is created to seed your config and is destroyed as soon as the connection is confirmed. After that, only Pi's standard auth store holds the key — exactly like a normal install, but proven working.

Stuck on step 2? The built-in key guide covers ten providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, DeepSeek, MiniMax, Mistral, Groq, OpenRouter, xAI, Ollama) — pricing, free tiers, the errors you'll hit.
