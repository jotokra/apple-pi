What it does

install.sh → model? → encrypt creds → confirm → DESTROY vault → hand off → agent asks to look around → tunes config to your model → offers a workflow

01   One-liner, then a wizard

Clone-and-run or curl-piped. Either way you land in a four-question wizard: model, provider, key (or skip for OAuth), passphrase. That's it.

02   Privacy by construction

  • Your credential is encrypted (openssl aes-256-cbc · pbkdf2 · 600k iters) into a transient vault.
  • The vault is destroyed the moment the model is confirmed.
  • What persists: your own config + auth.json + skills/prompts/extensions. Nothing else.
  • The shipped tree contains zero hostnames, IPs, paths, or tokens from the author — smoke/sanitize.sh grep-enforces it.

03   It tunes itself to your model

apple-pi is model-agnostic by design. Once your model is confirmed, a self-improvement ritual reads the model's real capabilities from the catalog + request-shaping code — not from prose — and retunes thinking levels, compaction, and favourites to match.

04   Then keeps improving (autoresearch)

An internal loop collects behavioral metrics daily and writes a weekly brief of proposed improvements. Nothing applies until you review it.

daily collect

LLM-free telemetry from your session logs → a local SQLite store. Zero quota burn.

weekly brief

Deltas vs last week + deterministic proposals. Surfaces tool-discipline drift, compaction pressure, error rates.

review → apply

apple-pi review shows the diff; apple-pi apply --latest --yes writes it. You gate every change.
